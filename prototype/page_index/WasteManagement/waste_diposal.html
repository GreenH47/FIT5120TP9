Waste Disposal Guide

1. Organic Waste

Organic waste includes food scraps, yard waste, and other biodegradable materials. It is recommended to compost organic waste to create nutrient-rich soil for gardening. Avoid throwing organic waste in regular garbage bins.

2. Plastic Waste

Plastic waste is a significant environmental concern. Separate plastic waste based on the recycling codes, if applicable, and dispose of them in the designated recycling bins. Avoid single-use plastics and opt for reusable alternatives whenever possible.

3. Paper and Cardboard

Paper and cardboard can be recycled. Flatten cardboard boxes before recycling. Remove any non-paper items, such as plastic windows, from paper products before recycling. Check with your local recycling facility for specific guidelines.

4. Hazardous Waste

Hazardous waste includes items like batteries, fluorescent bulbs, and electronic waste (e-waste). Properly dispose of hazardous waste at designated collection centers or follow local regulations. Do not dispose of hazardous waste in regular garbage bins.
